A quick analysis.

Looking at stock charts is fun, but perhaps the more interesting analysis is trying to accurately predict price movements.

There are a ton of approaches in attempting to forecast price movements in a stock, but one of the most basic is referred to as a "breakout", when a short-term simple moving average (SMA) of a stock price crosses a longer-term moving average, either in a positive or negative direction.

By selecting a ticker and clicking "Analyze" below, the 20-day and 50-day SMAs for the selected ticker will be charted. Take a look at the chart, and identify any points where the 20-Day SMA either crosses above or dips below the 50-Day SMA. If it crosses above, the SMA breakout strategy would predict the stock price should go up for some period. If it dips below, the strategy would expect the price to decrease for some period.

I think this goes without saying, but this should IN NO WAY be used as investment advice. You'd probably have a better shot at making money reading the latest post on r/wallstreetbets.
